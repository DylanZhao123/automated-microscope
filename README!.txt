Q: What if autofocus fails at both the original point and the four points at the corner?
A: The way to toggle between autofocus and manual focus is by pressing 'P' in the Remote APP. Try to press 'P' to toggle to manual focus, and after a few images, press 'P' to toggle back to manual focus. This may help.

Q: What if the camera is somehow far from focus?
A: You can try to adjust the height of the stage to "help" it a little bit.

Q: What if the program encounters any tough situation during the image-capturing process?
A: Move the mouse cursor to the left-bottom corner of the screen, and image capturing will terminate. After this, you will have to wait for 50 seconds before the detection thread terminates. After that, you can go on with the following code bars.

Q: What if the camera captures images that all look blurry even if auto-focus is on?
A: This is probably because the autofocus has "reached its limit". You can adjust the focus to a reasonable starting point by this: move the stage up (down) a little bit, and press '1' in Remote APP for a few seconds. If a clear image is successfully captured, do this a couple of times; if it takes a blurry image immediately, it means you are going the wrong direction. 

Q: What if jupyter notebook reports any error that cannot be solved?
A: Restart the notebook.

Q: What if the stage is not back at the origin after use (due to power failure or other reasons)
A: Go to Desktop\automated microscope\2DMatGMM-Main\demo\stage_adjustment.ipynb. Open the jupyter notebook and move the stage back to focus according to the instructions there.
